Arcangel & De La Ghetto – The Breakthrough Years

Arcangel’s career took off in 2004 when he formed the reggaeton duo Arcangel & De La Ghetto. They gained recognition for their hit single “Ven Pégate” and their appearances on popular reggaeton compilation albums such as “Sangre Nueva” and “Mas Flow: Los Benjamins.” While they never released a studio album, their collaborations and performances made a significant impact on the reggaeton scene.

In late 2006, Arcangel decided to pursue a solo career and founded his own record label, Flow Factory. Since then, he has released several successful albums, including “El Fenómeno” and “Sentimiento, Elegancia & Maldad,” and has collaborated with numerous renowned artists in the industry.

Arcangel’s Solo Career and International Success

Following his departure from Arcangel & De La Ghetto, Arcangel continued to make waves in the music industry as a solo artist. His debut solo album, “El Fenómeno,” was released in 2008 and featured collaborations with artists like Tempo, Don Omar, and J-King. The album reached number 14 on the Top Latin Albums chart and spawned hit singles such as “Por Amar a Ciegas” and “Pa’ Que la Pases Bien.”

Arcangel’s success extended beyond Puerto Rico, as he embarked on international tours and performed in countries like Germany, Spain, France, and Denmark. He captivated audiences with his electrifying performances and infectious energy, establishing a global fanbase. His talent and unique style resonated with people worldwide, allowing him to break cultural barriers and become a prominent figure in the international music scene.

Arcangel’s solo career continued to flourish with the release of subsequent albums, including “Ares,” “Historias de un Capricornio,” and “Sr. Santos.” Each album showcased his evolution as an artist and propelled him further into the spotlight. “Sr. Santos” featured the Latin hit “La Jumpa” in collaboration with the immensely popular Bad Bunny, further solidifying Arcangel’s status as a trailblazer in the genre.

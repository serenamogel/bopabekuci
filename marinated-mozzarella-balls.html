Marinated Mozzarella Balls

These marinated mozzarella balls are a quick and easy party appetizer! Coated in olive oil, lemon, garlic, and herbs, they're full of rich flavor.

Jump to recipe

These marinated mozzarella balls are quickly becoming a family favorite snack. I love them because they’re the perfect last-minute appetizer for gatherings—flavorful, elegant, and SO easy to throw together. Jack and Ollie love them…well…because they love anything with cheese.

The recipe is super simple. I marinate mini fresh mozzarella balls in olive oil with herbs, garlic, lemon zest, and red pepper flakes. The bright, savory flavor of the fresh garlic, lemon, and herbs saturates the oil, which in turn soaks into the chewy balls of cheese.

I hope these marinated mozzarella balls become your go-to appetizer for parties and gatherings, or a go-to snack to keep on hand in your fridge. They’re easy, delicious, and a little decadent. Enjoy!

How to Make Marinated Mozzarella Balls

This recipe starts with 8 simple ingredients:

Ingredients

  • Fresh mozzarella, of course! I recommend using ciliegine mozzarella for this recipe. These “cherry-sized” balls are perfect for eating in one bite! If you can’t find them, you can use bocconcini mozzarella balls or larger balls of fresh mozzarella cut into bite-sized pieces. Drain the mozzarella and pat it dry with a paper towel or kitchen towel before you make this recipe.
  • Extra-virgin olive oil – It creates the base of the marinade and adds rich, fruity flavor to the mozzarella.
  • Fresh herbs – For aromatic flavor! Fresh basil and parsley are my go-tos for this recipe. But for a fun twist, you could substitute half the amount of chopped fresh oregano or thyme.
  • Lemon juice and zest – They add bright, tangy flavor.
  • Garlic – For savory flavor.
  • Red pepper flakes – They add a kick of heat. Feel free to skip them if you’re sensitive to spice.
  • And sea salt – To make all the flavors pop!

Find the complete recipe with measurements below.

To make the marinated mozzarella balls, stir together the herbs, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, red pepper flakes, salt, and half the olive oil in a medium jar.

Add the cheese and top with the remaining oil, using more as needed to fully submerge the balls.

Cover and refrigerate for at least an hour. Finally, season to taste and serve!

How to Store

These marinated mozzarella balls keep well in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

The oil will solidify in the fridge, but don’t worry—it’s totally normal! Just let the marinated mozzarella sit at room temperature for a few minutes before serving. The oil will quickly return to its liquid state.

Marinated Mozzarella Balls

rate this recipe:5 from 2 votesPrep Time: 10 minutes minsMarinating Time: 1 hour hrTotal Time: 1 hour hr 10 minutes minsServes 4 to 6Save Recipe Print RecipeThese marinated mozzarella balls are an easy, make-ahead appetizer for parties and gatherings. Olive oil, lemon, garlic, and herbs fill them with rich flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley and/or basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest
  • 1 garlic clove, grated
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • ¼ teaspoon sea salt
  • ⅔ cup extra-virgin olive oil, or enough to cover the mozzarella balls
  • 8 ounces ciliegine fresh mozzarella cheese balls, drained and patted dry

Instructions

  • In a medium jar, stir together the herbs, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, red pepper flakes, salt, and ⅓ cup of the olive oil.
  • Add the mozzarella and top with the remaining ⅓ cup olive oil, adding more as needed to cover the mozzarella.
  • Cover and refrigerate for at least an hour before serving. Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

Allis Chalmers B Price, Specs, Review, Horsepower, Weight, Engine Oil Capacity,  Implements, History & Pictures

The Allis-Chalmers model B was a Row-Crop tractor manufactured by the Allis-Chalmers company from 1937 to 1957.

With a number of 125,000 units produced, Allis-Chalmers model B became one of the best-selling tractors for Allis-Chalmers and the most desired tractors of its time.

The Allis-Chalmers model B tractor was specially designed to enter into the small-farm market, and early literature focused on the market of using tractors instead of horses.

Allis Chalmers B Row-Crop Tractor is equipped with a Final engine, used from 1943 on, SN 64501 Allis-Chalmers CE gasoline 4-cylinder liquid-cooled 125.2 ci [2.1 L] engine, which delivers 19.51 hp (14.5 kW) power.

Allis Chalmers B Row-Crop Tractor featured with 4×2 2WD chassis, differential mechanical contracting band brakes, 3 forward and 1 reverse gear, and 2060 lbs weight.

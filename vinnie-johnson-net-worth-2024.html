Early Life and NBA Career

Vinnie Johnson was born on September 1, 1956, in Brooklyn, New York. He played college basketball at Baylor University before being drafted into the NBA in 1979. Johnson’s NBA career spanned over a decade, during which he earned the nickname “The Microwave” for his ability to score quickly off the bench. He is best known for his time with the Detroit Pistons, where he won two NBA championships.

Transition from Basketball to Business

After retiring from basketball in 1992, Johnson didn’t rest on his laurels. He quickly transitioned into the business world, founding Piston Group, an automotive supply company. His business acumen led to the company’s substantial growth, making it one of the largest African-American-owned businesses in the United States.

Vinnie Johnson’s Business Ventures

Johnson’s wealth is not solely from his NBA career; his business ventures have been a significant contributor. Piston Group has expanded to include Piston Automotive, Irvin Products, Detroit Thermal Systems, and AIREA. These subsidiaries have secured contracts with major automotive manufacturers, bolstering Johnson’s net worth.
